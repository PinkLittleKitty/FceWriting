FCE Writing

Introduction (1 paragraph)

  • Paraphrase the topic or question
  • Say what the essay will discuss
  • Mention the two or three main points

Example:

Many people believe that learning a second language is essential nowadays. In this essay, I will discuss the benefits of learning another language and whether schools should make it compulsory.

First Main Point (1 paragraph)

Start with a clear topic sentence and support with reasons, examples, or explanations.

Useful Phrases:

  • Firstly, ...
  • One important reason is that...
  • For example, ...

Second Main Point (1 paragraph)

Introduce the next idea clearly and support it with reasons or examples.

Useful Phrases:

  • Secondly, ...
  • Another important point is...
  • This means that...

Optional Third Main Point (1 paragraph)

Add a contrasting point or another supporting argument if needed.

Useful Phrases:

  • Finally, ...
  • On the other hand, ...
  • However, some people believe that...

Conclusion (1 paragraph)

  • Summarize the main points
  • Give your opinion (if asked)
  • Do not add new information

Useful Phrases:

  • In conclusion, ...
  • To sum up, ...
  • Personally, I believe that...

Introduction (1 paragraph)

  • Give basic information about what you're reviewing (title, creator, type)
  • Briefly explain what it's about and your overall impression

Useful Phrases:

  • The [book/movie/game/etc.] [Title], created by [Name], is about...
  • Overall, I found it to be...
  • In this review, I will talk about...

Example:

"Hollow Knight" is an indie metroidvania game developed by Team Cherry. It follows a mysterious warrior exploring a fallen insect kingdom. In this review, I'll talk about its atmosphere, gameplay, and why I think it stands out among indie titles.

Strengths (1 paragraph)

Discuss one or two things you really liked. Be specific with examples.

Useful Phrases:

  • One of the best things about this [item] is...
  • For example, the [graphics/dialogue/characters] really stood out
  • Another strong point is...

Weaknesses or Criticism (1 paragraph)

Mention anything that didn't work for you or could be improved.

Useful Phrases:

  • However, I found that...
  • One thing that could have been better is...
  • Some people might find that...

Optional Additional Thoughts (1 paragraph)

Include a personal reflection, comparison with similar titles, or something unique.

Useful Phrases:

  • Compared to other [games/books/etc.], this one...
  • What makes it different is...
  • Personally, I also enjoyed...

Conclusion (1 paragraph)

Summarize your overall opinion and say whether you'd recommend it.

Useful Phrases:

  • In conclusion, I think...
  • I would recommend this to...
  • Overall, it's a [fun/thought-provoking/well-made] experience

Introduction/Beginning (1 paragraph)

  • Set the scene and introduce main character(s)
  • Hint at the problem or situation they'll face

Useful Phrases:

  • It was a [cold/sunny/strange] day when...
  • [Character name] had no idea that today would change everything
  • The story begins in a [place] where...

Example:

It was a rainy afternoon when Mia found the old, locked box in her grandmother's attic. She didn't know it yet, but opening it would lead to a discovery no one could have imagined.

Build-Up/Rising Action (1-2 paragraphs)

  • Describe what happens next
  • Build tension or curiosity
  • Introduce the problem or challenge

Useful Phrases:

  • Everything seemed normal until...
  • Suddenly, [something unexpected] happened
  • As time passed, [character] began to realize...

Climax (1 paragraph)

  • The most exciting or intense part of the story
  • Describe how the character faces the challenge or problem

Useful Phrases:

  • At that moment,...
  • [Character] knew there was no turning back
  • With a deep breath, [he/she/they]...

Falling Action (1 paragraph)

  • Describe what happens after the climax
  • How does the character begin to solve things or return to normal?

Useful Phrases:

  • After that,...
  • Things slowly began to change
  • [Character] had learned...

Conclusion/Ending (1 paragraph)

  • Wrap up the story
  • Show what changed or what the character learned

Useful Phrases:

  • In the end,...
  • From that day on,...
  • [Character] would never forget...

Introduction - Hook the Reader

Start with a question, surprising fact, or bold opinion. Introduce the topic engagingly.

Useful Phrases:

  • Have you ever wondered...?
  • These days, many people...
  • It's hard to imagine life without...
  • In this article, I'll share...

Example:

Have you ever wondered what it's like to live without your phone for a week? I did it, and it completely changed the way I see technology.

Main Body (2-3 paragraphs)

Discuss your main points: personal experience, pros and cons, opinions, examples. Keep it informal or semi-formal. Use engaging language and direct questions to involve the reader.

Useful Phrases:

  • One of the best things about...
  • The worst part was...
  • For example,...
  • What's more,...
  • Another reason is...

Conclusion - Give Your Opinion

Wrap up with a strong final thought. Say what you learned or what you'd recommend.

Useful Phrases:

  • All in all,...
  • To sum up,...
  • In my opinion,...
  • I would definitely recommend...
  • If you ever get the chance,...
